## 1. What technologies are used?

| Type of technology | Description |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Cookies** | A cookie is a small file placed onto your device that enables Hatch features and functionality. We use two types of cookies: persistent cookies and session cookies. A **persistent cookie** lasts beyond the current session and is used for many purposes, such as recognizing you as an existing user, so it's easier to return to Hatch without signing in again. A **session cookie** lasts only as long as the session (usually the current visit to a website or a browser session). |
| **Pixels** | A pixel is a tiny image that may be embedded within web pages and emails, requiring a call to our servers to render. We use pixels to learn more about your interactions with email content or web content, such as whether you opened an email about a new Community or interacted with a specific job post. |
| **Local storage** | Local storage enables a website or application to store information locally on your device(s). We use local storage to improve the Hatch app experience-for example, by caching your Community feed, remembering your job preferences, and speeding up app functionality. |
| **Other similar technologies** | We also use other tracking technologies, such as mobile advertising IDs (since Hatch is accessible via mobile app) and tags for similar purposes as described in this policy. |

## 2. What are these technologies used for?

We use these technologies for the following purposes:

| Purpose | Description |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Authentication** | We use cookies and similar technologies to recognize you when you visit our Services. If you're signed into Hatch, these technologies help us show you the right information and personalize your experience. For example, cookies enable us to identify your role (Student, Founder, Professional, Freelancer) and verify your account. We also use cookies and secure tokens to facilitate **Single Sign-On (SSO)** via Google and Apple, allowing us to securely link your external social accounts with your Hatch profile and keep you logged in without requiring a password. |
| **Security** | We use cookies to make your interactions with our Services faster and more secure. For example, we use cookies to enable our security features, keep your account safe, and help us detect malicious activity and violations of our User Agreement. |
| **Preferences, features, and services** | We use cookies to enable the functionality of our Services, such as helping you fill out forms more easily (e.g., job applications or event RSVPs). We also use these technologies to remember information about your browser and your preferences, such as your preferred language or job search filters. |
| **Customized content** | We use cookies to customize your experience on our Services. For example, we may use cookies to remember previous searches so that when you return, we can offer additional Communities, Events, or Proof of Work portfolios that relate to your interests. |
| **Advertising** | Cookies and similar technologies help us show relevant opportunities to you more effectively. We may use these technologies to learn whether a job posting or event promotion has been shown to you, or whether you took an action (e.g., applied for a job or RSVP'd to an event). We may also work with our customers (like startups hiring on Hatch) to provide aggregated information about how users interact with their posts. |
| **Analytics and research** | Cookies and similar technologies help us learn more about how well our Services perform. We or our service providers use these technologies to understand, improve, and research products, features, and services. For example, we use them to see how members navigate through the "Proof of Work" portfolios or how often they engage with Community chats. |

## 4. Your Choices

You have choices on how Hatch uses cookies and similar technologies. Please note that if you limit the ability of Hatch to set cookies and similar technologies, you may worsen your overall user experience, since it may no longer be personalized to you. It may also stop you from saving customized settings like login information.

### Browser Controls
Most browsers allow you to control cookies through their settings. You can typically review and erase cookies, including Hatch cookies. To learn more about browser controls, please consult the documentation that your browser manufacturer provides.

### Mobile Device Controls
Some mobile device operating systems (such as iOS and Android) provide the ability to control the use of mobile advertising IDs for ad personalization. You can learn how to use these controls by visiting your device manufacturer's website or adjusting the "Privacy" or "Ads" settings on your mobile device.

### In-App Controls
Where applicable, Hatch Members can adjust their data usage and privacy preferences directly within the **Settings & Privacy** section of the Hatch app.

## 5. What is Do Not Track (DNT)?

DNT is a concept that has been promoted by regulatory agencies for the Internet industry to develop and implement a mechanism for allowing Internet users to control the tracking of their online activities across websites. Currently, there is no universally accepted standard for handling DNT signals. As such, Hatch does not generally respond to "do not track" signals.
