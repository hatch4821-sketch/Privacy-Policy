# Hatch Professional Community Policies

**Effective Date:** [Date, e.g., April 1, 2024]

### We want Hatch to reflect the best version of the builder and professional ecosystem.
Thank you for using Hatch. This is a community where students, founders, freelancers, and professionals come together to showcase their "Proof of Work," collaborate in Communities, learn, and unlock economic opportunity.

To make this possible, the content you contribute should add to the Hatch community in a constructive manner. We treat each other with respect, provide honest feedback, and help each other succeed. Together, we can make our community a place where everyone is able to learn, build, and grow.

### Tell us if you see abusive content
If you see something you believe may violate our policies-whether in profiles, portfolios, community chats, event pages, or anywhere else-please report it to us at **[Insert Support Email, e.g., support@hatch.com]** or by using our in-app reporting tools.

**Reporting Violations:** We rely on our community to keep Hatch safe. You can report a User, Post, or Community using our in-app reporting tool. When reporting, please select the appropriate category: **Spam, Harassment, Inappropriate Content, Misinformation, or Other**, and provide a detailed description of the issue.

### Violating our community policies has consequences
These policies apply to all Members. Depending on the severity of the violation, we may limit the visibility of certain content, remove it entirely, or restrict/terminate your account. Repeated or egregious offenses will result in a permanent ban.

---

## 1. Be Safe
*Only bring safe conversations to Hatch.*

**Do not post harassing content:**
We do not tolerate bullying or harassment. Because Hatch is a place where Members share their early-stage projects, code, and ideas, constructive criticism is welcome, but tearing others down is not. This includes targeted personal attacks, intimidation, shaming, disparagement, and abusive language. Do not reveal others' personal or sensitive information (doxing), or incite others to do the same.

**Do not threaten, incite, or promote violence:**
We do not allow threatening or inciting violence of any kind. We do not allow individuals or groups that engage in or promote violence, property damage, or organized criminal activity on our platform.

**Do not share restricted, dangerous, or illegal material:**
We do not allow content that facilitates the purchase or sale of dangerous or illegal goods, scraped data, or proxy test-taking services. Do not use Hatch to organize, depict, or facilitate criminal activity. We have zero tolerance for the exploitation of children or human trafficking.

---

## 2. Be Trustworthy
*Authenticity is everything. Use your true identity, claim only the work you actually did, and share information that is real.*

**Do not plagiarize or fake your "Proof of Work":**
Hatch is built on showing what you can actually do. Do not upload projects, code, designs, or portfolios that belong to someone else and claim them as your own. If you collaborated on a project, clearly state your specific role. Do not distribute fake educational or professional certifications.

**Do not share false or misleading content:**
Do not share content that is false, misleading, or intended to deceive. Be transparent if your "Proof of Work" relies heavily on AI-generated outputs, templates, or open-source material-give credit where it is due. Do not share synthetic or manipulated media (deepfakes) that wrongfully depicts a person saying or doing something they did not do.

**Do not create a fake profile or falsify your background:**
We do not allow fake profiles or entities. Do not post misleading or deceptive information about yourself, your startup, your freelance business, your qualifications, or your achievements. Do not use an image of someone else for your profile photo. Do not associate yourself with a business, college, or startup that you are not actually associated with.

**Do not scam, defraud, or deceive others:**
Do not use Hatch to promote pyramid schemes, fake startup investment opportunities, or fraudulent freelance gigs. Do not share malicious software that puts our Members, platform, or services at risk (e.g., hiding malware in a portfolio link). Phishing attempts are strictly prohibited.

**Do not post fake jobs or deceptive company profiles:**
If you are managing a Company profile or posting a Job (Full-time, Contract, Internship, or Freelance), the information must be accurate. Do not post "ghost" jobs, pyramid schemes, or unpaid roles disguised as paid opportunities. Do not misrepresent your startup's investment stage or employee count.

---

## 3. Be Professional
*We allow broad conversations about the world of building, startups, and work, but require professional expression. Treat each other with civility.*

**Do not be hateful:**
We do not allow content that attacks, denigrates, intimidates, dehumanizes, or incites hatred against individuals or groups based on their actual or perceived race, ethnicity, national origin, caste, gender, gender identity, sexual orientation, religious affiliation, age, or disability status. Hate groups are not permitted on Hatch.

**Do not engage in unwanted romantic advances:**
Hatch is a professional networking and portfolio platform, not a dating app. Do not use Hatch to pursue romantic connections, ask for dates, or provide sexual commentary on someone's appearance. Do not send unwanted advances in Community chats, direct messages, or comments.

**Do not share shocking, graphic, or sexually explicit material:**
We do not allow content that is excessively gruesome, sadistic, or gratuitously graphic. Do not share material depicting nudity or sexual activity.

**Do not spam Members or the platform:**
We do not allow untargeted, irrelevant, or gratuitously repetitive messages. Do not use our platform to send mass promotional messages to people you don't know, or flood Community feeds with irrelevant links. Do not artificially inflate engagement on your Proof of Work or posts by trading likes, using bots, or organizing engagement pods. Make the effort to create original, relevant, and interesting content to gain real engagement.

**Respect messaging limits and group chats:**
Group chats are capped at 100 members to ensure quality conversations. Do not spam group or community chats with excessive audio messages, large file dumps, or unsolicited promotional links.
